Details

This article describes the battle between the students of the Faculty of Architecture and the armed forces in Rome, Italy on March 1st, 1968. The event is frequently associated and remembered as one of the first violent clashes in Italy's student unrest during the protests of 1968. In the aftermath of the clash that began with 4,000 students gathering in the Piazza di Spagna, 148 injuries were to police, 478 injuries to students, four of which were detained, and 228 were arrested.

Item information

  • Category: Press article
  • Date: 13/12/1969
  • URL: Click here

Details

This article relates to the terrorist attack that occurred on December 12th, 1969 at the headquarters of the National Agriculture Bank in Piazza Fontana in Milan, Italy. The bombing resulted in the deaths of 17 people and injured 88 others. In the same afternoon, three more bombings occurred in Rome and Milan, and an additional bomb was found unexploded. It was later discovered that the attack was carried out by the far-right, neo-fascist terrorist group Ordine Nuovo, as well as other unknown collaborators.

Item information

  • Category Press article
  • Date 14/12/1969
  • URL: Click here

Details

This article is part of an inquiry run by The Observer about the connections between italian and greek right parties in 1969. Two days after the Piazza Fontana Bombing (14 December 1969) the term “Strategy of tension” appeared for the first time exactly in this article, as a reference probably to the turbulent political climate of those days in Italy. Neal Ascherson, one of the authors of that article, during an interview in 2014 said that particular expression was suggested by an italian colleague in contact whit him before and after the Piazza Fontana Bombing.

Details

The song by Lucio Dalla is part of the album Dalla (september 1980), and its lyrics talk about the terrorist attack in Bologna Central Station on August 2nd, 1980. The “dancer dancing on the plain between the mountains” is aksed to stop the train traveling from Frankfurt to Palermo, the same that blew up during the attack and mentioned in the song. This song can be considered a poetic memorial to that event.

  • Category Multimedia Material
  • Date 14/5/1977
  • URL: Click here

Details

The photo by Paolo Pedrizzetti, representing a young man in the act of shooting, became the symbol of the armed struggle of the Years of the Lead. The man in the picture is Giuseppe Memeo, who initially was charged with the homicide of the policeman Antonino Custra. On 14th May 1977, the policeman was killed in via De Amicis, Milan. Only in May 1992, the Assize Court sentenced nine people for the facts of May 14th, 1977. At that point, it was clear that Memeo was not the murderer of Custra.

Item information

  • Category Text document
  • Date 2007
  • URL: Click here

Details

The book by Mario Calabresi tells the story of the murder of Luigi Calabresi, his father, who was a police Chief Commissioner in Milan. He was killed by a terrorist attack of the extreme left movements in 1972. Between 1969 and 1972, he was accused by the extreme left of murdering the anarchist Giuseppe Pinelli, who died after falling from a fourth-floor window of the Milan Police Headquarters. Pinelli was in custody for the investigation into the Piazza Fontana massacre. The accusations came from an extensive press campaign by political left-wing newspapers, in particular L'Espresso and the newspaper of the extreme left-wing militant movement Lotta Continua.

Details

This report relates to the Parlamentary Inquiry that have been run by a special Commission during the 80s in order to inquire into Masonic Lodge P2, a secret criminal organization responsible for different illegal activities (the kidnapping of Aldo Moro) and violent attacks against the Italian state such the Train station Bologna Massacre . The report was supervised by Tina Anselmi, nominated President of the Commission from 1981 to 1985, and it is part of a documentary collection about the P2 held by the “Rete degli Archivi per non dimenticare” institution.
